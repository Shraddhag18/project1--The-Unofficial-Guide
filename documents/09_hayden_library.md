# Hayden Library, Tempe Campus

Hayden Library is the main library on the Tempe campus, holding 4+ million
volumes across all disciplines.

## What's on each level
- **Concourse:** information and check-out, tutoring and academic support,
  classrooms (C1–C34), Charlie's Cafe, reservable wellness and lactation rooms,
  an interfaith reflection room, and an ablution room.
- **Lower Level:** current journals, videos, the East Asian Collection, and the
  Latin Americana Collection, plus classrooms L50, L60, and L62.
- **Level One:** the Wurzburger Reading Room and the Luhrs Arizona Reading Room,
  featured collections, a Distinctive Collections instruction room, University
  Librarian offices, and Bodhi Market.
- **Level Two:** book stacks (Sun Devil Reads, Labriola Open Stack, featured
  collections), the Zine Collection, the Labriola National American Indian Data
  Center, instruction room 236, and study areas.
- **Level Three:** the Makerspace, the Map and Geospatial Hub, Naturespace, the
  Unit for Data Science and Analytics, instruction rooms 317 and 332, media lab
  354, and group study rooms.
- **Level Four:** book stacks (A–F, P, U–V), the Scholar's Enclave, featured
  collections, and study spaces.

## Study rooms
Hayden offers collaborative group study rooms, two reading rooms, and a variety
of research services. Study rooms can be reserved online through the library's
LibCal system for up to four hours per day per person.

## Hours
During fall and spring semesters the library is generally open until midnight
Sunday–Thursday, with 24/7 access during finals weeks. Summer hours are shorter.
Note that the Noble Library (also on the Tempe campus) is the location open 24
hours, five days a week.
