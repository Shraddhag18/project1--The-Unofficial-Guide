# Getting Involved — Clubs and Student Organizations

## How many clubs, and where to find them
ASU has more than 1,000 student organizations across its four campuses. Browse
organizations and upcoming events on Sun Devil Sync (formerly SunDevilSync),
ASU's online student-group management system. Sun Devil Central is the platform
used to register and manage organizations.

## How to join
Find a club on Sun Devil Sync and request to join through the platform. Getting
involved is pitched as a way to build lifelong friendships, grow personally,
develop leadership skills, and engage in community service.

## Membership requirement
To be a member of a student organization you must be enrolled in a minimum of 3
credit hours. If you want to join but don't meet that requirement, you can
submit an exception request form.

## Involvement fairs
ASU hosts involvement fairs each semester — Fall Forward in the fall and the
Spring Involvement Fair in the spring — where organizations table on campus so
students can sign up in person.

## Questions
For Tempe-campus club questions, email tempeclubs@asu.edu. The Student
Organizations and Leadership office (within Educational Outreach and Student
Services) supports registered student organizations.
