# Sun Devil Fitness Complex (SDFC), Tempe Campus

The SDFC opened on August 21, 1989 as the Student Recreation Complex and was
expanded and modernized in 2013.

## Access / membership
All fee-paying ASU students are automatically members of the Sun Devil Fitness
Complex through their ASU registration fees, with access to SDFC facilities and
programs on all campuses. Membership covers use of equipment and participation
in programs and services.

## Weights and cardio
- Two weight rooms plus a circuit-training room
- Over 100 pieces of cardio equipment: stationary bikes, upper-body ergometers,
  stair steppers, elliptical trainers, treadmills, and rowing machines

## Aquatics
- A swimming pool holding 900,000 gallons, with a heating system

## Courts and recreation spaces
- A three-court gymnasium, a two-court gymnasium, and a multi-activity court
  (MAC) gymnasium
- Basketball, racquetball, volleyball, badminton courts, and one squash court
- Outdoor sand volleyball and two tennis courts (near Sonora Hall)
- Ping pong and foosball tables

## Other facilities
- Multipurpose exercise rooms and two group-fitness studios
- A wellness suite with massage therapy, a meditation room, and a think-tank room
- Lighted outdoor fields
- An outdoor running track (about 0.44 miles per lap) and an indoor track
  (10 laps = 1 mile)
- Men's and women's locker rooms, each with 1,000 lockers, showers, and bathrooms
- An equipment rental room and a Bike Co-Op
- Conference rooms and classrooms

Typical hours are Monday–Friday 6 a.m.–11 p.m. and Saturday–Sunday 9 a.m.–10
p.m., varying by season; the complex is closed on major holidays.
