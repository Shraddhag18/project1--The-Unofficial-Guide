# A Student's Practical Guide to ASU Tempe Parking

This is the off-the-record version of campus parking — what actually works day
to day, from a student-housing perspective.

## Which lots students actually use
Lot 59, Lot 45, and Lot 3 are the go-to commuter areas because they are the
cheapest student-accessible options. If you want covered parking to beat the
heat, the Apache Boulevard, Rural Road, and Stadium garages have shade but cost
more. Permits generally range from about $210 to $780 per academic year
depending on the zone — always re-check current rates each year on ASU's site.

## Timing is everything
Plan to arrive before 9:00 AM for the best chance at an open spot. Avoid
arriving around noon, when classes change over and lots fill fast. Check ASU
alerts during major events (football games, performances) because parking gets
restricted.

## Skip parking entirely
- Valley Metro light rail and buses reach campus and downtown Tempe directly.
- Biking is genuinely viable on the flat Tempe campus and often faster than
  hunting for a space.
- Some apartment communities run their own free shuttles to campus on school
  days (for example, every 30 minutes from 7:30 AM to 5:30 PM), sharing stops
  with ASU's intercampus shuttles.

## Apps and tools
ParkMobile and SpotHero help you find open spots and pay digitally. Use ASU's
online parking portal to buy and manage permits. Street parking near campus is
limited and usually metered or time-restricted, so don't count on it.

## Bottom line
Always display your permit properly and park only in your assigned zone —
citations are common and add up fast.
